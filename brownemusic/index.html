Browne's Story

Herschel Lee Brown, known as Browne by his friends and family, is a Southern singer/songwriter who attributes his first musical influences to the soulful voices of relatives singing around the old upright piano in his Grandparents home in Sharon, South Carolina, ten miles from where his family first settled on the Broad River in 1754. Browne's voice is his best instrument and has been described as "powerful yet soothing-edgy and soft-soaring and sweet."

The familiarity of his lyrics and powerful vocal style reflect Browne's authentically American musical influences, which include old Southern hymns, country and bluegrass from the Grand Ole Opry, and folk rock artists of the late sixties and seventies including John Denver, John Prine, Bob Dylan, Dan Fogelberg, Joni Mitchell, Emmy Lou Harris, and Crosby-Stills-Nash and Young.

Like many children of that era, Browne sang and played guitar in various bands throughout his late teens and early 20's but life happens, and he had to put down his guitar and grab upa hammer to support and raise his family. Now that his children have grown children of their own, he has picked up where he left off, creating his own unique blend of alternative country and Americana music.

Browne's friends and family still gather and sing around that very same old upright piano, which now resides in his home music studio.
